Common Issues Guide

Device Outputs Blank Pages — Why and How to Fix It

Step-by-step diagnosis and fix.

What Causes It

Blank pages happen when the device is told to print something but the page never receives ink. The most common causes are an empty cartridge that still reports as having ink, a clogged print head, or the wrong driver sending an empty job.

Less commonly, a partial document or a driver bug can produce blank output even with a working device.

Step-by-Step Fix

Work through the steps in order — most cases resolve at step two or three.

  • Print the device's self-test page — From the device menu, run a self-test. If the test prints, the device is fine and the issue is the driver or the document. If the test is blank, the issue is the device.
  • Run a nozzle check — On inkjets, run a nozzle check from the maintenance menu. Blank or banded output means the heads are clogged — run a cleaning cycle.
  • Check ink or toner levels — Replace any cartridge below 15%. Some inkjet cartridges report level inaccurately and run dry without warning.
  • Try a different document — Print a plain text file. If it works, the original document is the issue.
  • Re-add the driver — An partial driver can send empty pages. Remove and re-add the latest from the vendor.

Why It Matters

Resolving this at the right level — driver, queue or hardware — saves you from chasing the same fault every few weeks. Misdiagnosis (treating a hardware issue as software, or vice versa) is what makes device issues look intermittent.

When to Contact Support

If the issue returns within 24 hours of completing every step above, the underlying cause is likely hardware. At that point a manufacturer warranty claim or a visit to a local repair specialist is worth more than another driver re-add. Contact us for a second opinion before paying for a repair.
