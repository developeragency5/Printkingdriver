Troubleshooting Guide

Print Spooler Service Keeps Stopping — Permanent Fix

Step-by-step diagnosis and fix.

What Causes It

A repeatedly crashing Print Spooler is almost always caused by a corrupted print driver or a stuck spool file the spooler cannot recover from.

Each restart loads the corrupted state again, the spooler crashes again, and the cycle repeats. Breaking the cycle requires removing the corrupted item permanently.

Step-by-Step Fix

Work through the steps in order — most cases resolve at step two or three.

  • Stop the spooler — services.msc » Print Spooler » Stop.
  • Clear all spool files — Delete every file in C:\Windows\System32\spool\PRINTERS.
  • Remove every printer from the queue list — Settings » Printers, remove each one. Yes, all of them — you can re-add the working ones afterward.
  • Remove every driver from Print Management — Print Management » All Drivers, delete each entry.
  • Restart the spooler and re-add printers one at a time — After each addition, leave it for a minute and confirm the spooler is still running before adding the next.

Why It Matters

Resolving this at the right level — driver, queue or hardware — saves you from chasing the same fault every few weeks. Misdiagnosis (treating a hardware issue as software, or vice versa) is what makes printer issues look intermittent.

When to Contact Support

If the issue returns within 24 hours of completing every step above, the underlying cause is likely hardware. At that point a manufacturer warranty claim or a visit to a local repair specialist is worth more than another driver reinstall. Contact us for a second opinion before paying for a repair.
