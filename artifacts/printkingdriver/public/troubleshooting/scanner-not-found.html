Common Issues Guide

Scanner Not Detected — How to Recover the Connection

When the device prints fine but the scanner is invisible to the operating system.

What Causes It

On most multi-function devices the device and scanner are exposed to the operating system as two separate devices, each with its own driver. The print driver is small and lightweight and is often what gets installed automatically over the network. The scan driver — TWAIN on PC, ICA on macOS, SANE on Linux — is a separate package and is often skipped during a quick install.

If you can print but cannot scan, the print driver was installed but the scan driver was not. Reinstalling the full vendor driver package usually fixes it.

Step-by-Step Fix

Work through the steps in order. Most cases resolve at step two or three; the later steps cover stubborn problems.

  • Confirm the device is reachable — If you can print, the network or USB connection itself is fine. If you cannot, fix the device offline issue first.
  • Reinstall the full driver package — Visit the manufacturer support site, search the exact device model, choose the "Full driver and software" option (not the print-only one) and run the installer.
  • Add the scanner manually — On PC, open Device Manager » Imaging Devices and use Add Legacy Hardware. On macOS, open Image Capture and check whether the device appears under "Shared".
  • Restart the scan service — On PC, restart the PC Image Acquisition (WIA) service. On macOS, log out and back in to refresh the ICA service.
  • Check vendor app — Many manufacturers ship a dedicated scan app (the manufacturer's Smart, the manufacturer Scan 2, the manufacturer IJ Scan Utility). The OS scan dialog may fail while the vendor app works — try the vendor app first to confirm the scanner itself is fine.

Why It Matters

Resolving this at the right level — driver, queue or hardware — saves you from chasing the same fault every few weeks. The wrong fix (for example reinstalling the driver when the real problem is a stuck job) makes the issue look intermittent and harder to diagnose later.

When to Contact Support

If the problem returns within 24 hours of completing every step above, the underlying cause is likely a hardware fault — a worn pickup roller, a failing print head or a logic-board issue. At that point it is worth reaching out to the manufacturer's warranty support, or to our team for a second opinion before paying for a repair.
