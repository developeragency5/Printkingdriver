Setup Guide

AirPrint and Mopria

Everything that has to line up for a airprint and mopria to work first time, and what to do when one of those things does not.

Quick Summary

AirPrint (Apple) and Mopria (Android) are driverless printing standards built into modern printers and operating systems. They allow any compatible phone, tablet, or laptop to print over Wi-Fi without installing a printer driver. Over 10,000 printer models support at least one of these standards in 2026.

  • AirPrint is built into every iPhone, iPad, and Mac since 2010 — no app or driver required
  • Mopria Print Service is pre-installed on most Android phones and also available on Windows
  • Both standards require the printer and device to be on the same Wi-Fi network

Overview

AirPrint and Mopria are vendor-neutral standards built on top of IPP Everywhere-Everywhere. They let an iPhone, iPad, Mac or Android device print to any compatible printer without installing a vendor driver or app. If a printer was made in the last five years, it almost certainly supports both.

Step-by-Step

  • Check the printer's certification — Look on the box or vendor support page for the AirPrint or Mopria logo. Both standards use the same underlying technology.
  • Connect printer and device to the same Wi-Fi — Both standards use mDNS (Bonjour) to advertise the printer. The advertisement is only visible inside the local network.
  • Print from the device — On iOS or macOS, choose Share » Print and pick the printer. On Android, choose Print from any sharing menu and select the printer from the Mopria-discovered list.
  • No driver install needed — AirPrint and Mopria use a fixed standard driver model. The OS already knows how to talk to the printer.
  • What you lose — Vendor-specific features (paper-size shortcuts, ink-level monitor, finishing options) are not exposed through AirPrint or Mopria. For those, install the vendor app instead.

What Can Go Wrong

A short list of the issues people most often run into during airprint and mopria, and the right fix for each:

SymptomLikely CauseFirst Step
Connection succeeds but no printDriver mismatched to the model.Reinstall the vendor driver.
Connection drops after a few minutesPower-saving or sleep settings.Disable sleep on the printer or computer.
Printer not discoveredDiscovery protocol blocked or different subnet.Confirm both devices on same network.
