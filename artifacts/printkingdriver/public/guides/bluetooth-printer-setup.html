Setup Guide

Bluetooth Device Setup

Everything that has to line up for a bluetooth device setup to work first time, and what to do when one of those things does not.

Quick Summary

Bluetooth device setup pairs a device directly to a single device without needing a Wi-Fi network or USB cable. The range is limited to around ten metres and the connection is to one device at a time. Bluetooth is most useful for laptops, tablets, and phones when a network is not available.

  • Turn on Bluetooth on both the device and the computer before starting
  • Bluetooth connections are one-to-one — only one device prints at a time
  • Range is typically ten metres; walls and interference can reduce this further

Overview

Bluetooth printing is most common on portable receipt and label devices, and a few mobile-targeted inkjet models. It is slow and short-range compared to Wi-Fi, but it requires no network at all, which makes it useful for ad-hoc and field use.

Step-by-Step

  • Pair the device — On the device, enable Bluetooth pairing mode. On the computer or phone, choose Add Bluetooth Device and pick the device from the list.
  • Enter the PIN if prompted — Many Bluetooth devices have a default PIN of 0000 or 1234. Check the device manual.
  • Set Up the driver — For one-off paired use the OS may set up a generic driver. For regular use set up the vendor driver, which adds support for the device's actual paper sizes.
  • Confirm the range — Bluetooth is reliable up to about 10 metres line-of-sight. Walls and metal cabinets reduce the range significantly.
  • Repair if dropped — If the connection drops, remove the device and re-pair from scratch. Bluetooth pairing data sometimes gets incomplete after a device firmware update.

What Can Go Wrong

A short list of the issues people most often run into during bluetooth device setup, and the right fix for each:

SymptomLikely CauseFirst Step
Connection succeeds but no printDriver mismatched to the model.Re-add the vendor driver.
Connection drops after a few minutesPower-saving or sleep settings.Disable sleep on the device or computer.
Device not discoveredDiscovery protocol blocked or different subnet.Confirm both devices on same network.
