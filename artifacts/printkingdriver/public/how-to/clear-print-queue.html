How to Clear a Stuck Print Queue

A stuck queue blocks every job sent after it. Clearing it is fast once you know the right sequence.

Quick Summary

Clearing a stuck print queue removes jobs that will not delete through the normal interface. This is usually caused by a partial job file or an unresponsive Print Spooler service. This how-to explains three methods in order of effort: through PC Settings, by restarting the Print Spooler, and by manually deleting spool files.

  • Vendor server outage — vendor support sites occasionally go down for hours. Try again later or check the vendor status page.

Why It Matters

Each of these "small" tasks is a daily friction point that compounds over time. Five minutes lost per print job, ten times a day, costs you several hours a month. Doing it right once and saving the procedure is worth the upfront effort.
