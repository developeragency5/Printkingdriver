Print Technology

Laser vs Inkjet Printers

A clear explanation of laser vs inkjet printers for non-engineers.

Overview

Laser and inkjet are the two dominant printing technologies for home and office. They produce broadly similar output but the underlying technology, running cost and maintenance routine are completely different.

How Each One Works

An inkjet printer sprays microscopic droplets of liquid ink through hundreds of tiny nozzles in a moving print head. The droplets land on the page and the ink soaks into the paper fibres.

A laser printer instead uses a static-charged drum, a laser to "draw" the page on the drum, and dry toner powder that sticks to the charged areas. The toner is then transferred to the page and fused in place by a hot roller called the fuser.

Running Cost

Inkjet ink is expensive per page; laser toner is cheaper per page but the cartridges cost more upfront. Refillable-tank inkjets (EcoTank, MegaTank) flip the economics — their per-page cost is cheaper than laser for most workloads.

Print Quality

Inkjet wins on photos and rich colour gradients. Laser wins on text sharpness, consistency across long jobs, and resistance to smudging.

Speed

Laser is faster across the board, especially for the second and subsequent pages. Inkjet pauses between pages as the print head returns and waits for the previous sheet to dry.

Maintenance

Inkjet needs regular use to keep the print head from clogging — leave it for a month and you will spend ink on cleaning cycles. Laser tolerates long idle periods well.

Bottom Line

Pick laser for high-volume office use and consistent text. Pick inkjet for occasional photo printing and home use, ideally a refillable-tank model to keep running cost down.
