The manufacturer's Device Drivers

Where to find the manufacturer drivers, what each one does and how to keep them working reliably.

About the manufacturer

The manufacturer makes one of the broadest device ranges available, from the entry-level DeskJet and ENVY home models through the OfficeJet and PageWide small-business series to the LaserJet enterprise line. Their devices ship in nearly every region and are commonly bundled with new computers.

How the manufacturer Distributes Drivers

The manufacturer offers two distinct driver styles. The "the manufacturer's Smart" experience is a modern, lightweight setup tool that pairs the device automatically and registers it with the operating system's print stack. The classic "Full Feature Software" still exists for older models and includes the device panel, ink-level monitor and faxing tools.

For any the manufacturer model, the safest place to start is the official the manufacturer support site. Search by exact model number, choose your operating system and download the most recent stable driver. Avoid third-party "driver updater" tools — they often bundle older or repackaged setup tools that miss security fixes.

Choosing the Right the manufacturer Driver

The manufacturer typically lists several download options for the same device. The choice depends on what you need:

  • Full driver and software package — the complete setup tool with the driver, device support, status monitor and any branded apps. Best for everyday users.
  • Output-only driver — a minimal setup tool that adds the device to the system's job queue without extra utilities. Best for shared office machines.
  • PostScript or PCL driver — page-description-language variants used in design studios and large workgroups. Choose PostScript for professional graphics, PCL for general office use.

Best Practices

A short checklist to keep your the manufacturer's device healthy and your driver in good shape.

  • Always download from the manufacturer's own support website rather than third-party mirrors.
  • Match the driver to the exact model number printed on the device — similar models often need different drivers.
  • Keep the firmware on the device itself up to date; firmware and driver are designed as a pair.
  • Reboot the computer after a major driver upgrade to release the old kernel-mode print module.
