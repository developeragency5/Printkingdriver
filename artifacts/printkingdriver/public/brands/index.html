Device Brand Driver Guides

Where to find official drivers for the most popular device manufacturers, what each one offers and how to choose between them.

Why a Brand-Specific Page?

Every device manufacturer organises its driver downloads, set up routines and companion utilities differently. A driver that works perfectly on one brand will not even set up on another. The pages below explain the practical differences — setup tool style, driver naming, where to find the model number, what to expect during set up — for the brands you are most likely to own.

Not Sure Which Brand You Have?

The brand and model are usually printed on a small label on the front of the device, near the paper tray or under the lid. The label on the back is also reliable. The model number printed on the box can be a marketing name and may not match what the driver download page expects — always use the label on the device itself.

What If My Brand Is Not Listed?

The ten brands above cover roughly 95% of the devices in everyday use, but there are dozens of smaller manufacturers. If yours is not listed, the general advice is the same: download the latest driver directly from the manufacturer support site, match the exact model number, and prefer the full driver package over the print-only version if you also need to scan or fax. Contact us if you would like a guide for a specific brand.
