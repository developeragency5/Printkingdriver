Printer Brand Guide

HP Printer Drivers

Where to find HP drivers, what each one does and how to keep them working reliably.

About HP

HP makes one of the broadest printer ranges available, from the entry-level DeskJet and ENVY home models through the OfficeJet and PageWide small-business series to the LaserJet enterprise line. Their devices ship in nearly every region and are commonly bundled with new computers.

Founded1939
Head officePalo Alto, USA
Main printer linesDeskJet, OfficeJet, ENVY, LaserJet, PageWide

How HP Distributes Drivers

HP offers two distinct driver styles. The "HP Smart" experience is a modern, lightweight installer that pairs the device automatically and registers it with the operating system's print stack. The classic "Full Feature Software" still exists for older models and includes the scanner panel, ink-level monitor and faxing tools.

For any HP model, the safest place to start is the official HP support site. Search by exact model number, choose your operating system and download the most recent stable driver. Avoid third-party "driver updater" tools — they often bundle older or repackaged installers that miss security fixes.

Choosing the Right HP Driver

HP typically lists several download options for the same printer. The choice depends on what you need:

  • Full driver and software package — the complete installer with the driver, scanner support, status monitor and any branded apps. Best for everyday users.
  • Print-only driver — a minimal installer that adds the device to the system's print queue without extra utilities. Best for shared office machines.
  • PostScript or PCL driver — page-description-language variants used in design studios and large workgroups. Choose PostScript for professional graphics, PCL for general office use.
  • Universal driver — a single driver that works across many HP models. Useful for managing a mixed fleet from one Windows print server.

Common HP Driver Issues

SymptomLikely CauseFirst Step
Printer offline after a Windows updateThe new operating system replaced the driver with a generic one.Reinstall the latest HP driver from the official site.
Print queue stuck or pausedA previous print job did not clear from the spooler.Cancel all jobs, restart the print spooler service, then retry.
Wireless printer not foundThe device is on a different subnet or guest network.Confirm the printer and computer are on the same Wi-Fi network.
Scanner not recognisedThe scanner driver was not installed alongside the print driver.Install the full HP driver package, not the print-only version.

Best Practices

A short checklist to keep your HP printer healthy and your driver in good shape.

  • Always download from HP's own support website rather than third-party mirrors.
  • Match the driver to the exact model number printed on the device — similar models often need different drivers.
  • Install Windows updates first, then the HP driver, so the driver loads against the current print stack.
  • Keep the firmware on the printer itself up to date; firmware and driver are designed as a pair.
  • Reboot the computer after a major driver upgrade to release the old kernel-mode print module.
