Ricoh Printer Drivers

Where to find Ricoh drivers, what each one does and how to keep them working reliably.

About Ricoh

Ricoh is a major player in the office multi-function market, particularly in Europe and Asia. The IM and MP families are mid- to high-volume office devices with rich finishing options, while the SP and P lines cover desktop printing.

Founded1936
Head officeTokyo, Japan
Main printer linesIM, MP, SP, P

How Ricoh Distributes Drivers

Ricoh provides a Universal Print Driver for Windows that supports most of their colour and mono fleet from one installer, plus dedicated PCL6 and PostScript drivers per model. The Smart Device Monitor companion app handles status reporting and SNMP discovery on a network.

For any Ricoh model, the safest place to start is the official Ricoh support site. Search by exact model number, choose your operating system and download the most recent stable driver. Avoid third-party "driver updater" tools — they often bundle older or repackaged installers that miss security fixes.

Choosing the Right Ricoh Driver

Ricoh typically lists several download options for the same printer. The choice depends on what you need:

  • Full driver and software package — the complete installer with the driver, scanner support, status monitor and any branded apps. Best for everyday users.
  • Print-only driver — a minimal installer that adds the device to the system's print queue without extra utilities. Best for shared office machines.
  • PostScript or PCL driver — page-description-language variants used in design studios and large workgroups. Choose PostScript for professional graphics, PCL for general office use.
  • Universal driver — a single driver that works across many Ricoh models. Useful for managing a mixed fleet from one Windows print server.

Common Ricoh Driver Issues

SymptomLikely CauseFirst Step
Printer offline after a Windows updateThe new operating system replaced the driver with a generic one.Reinstall the latest Ricoh driver from the official site.
Print queue stuck or pausedA previous print job did not clear from the spooler.Cancel all jobs, restart the print spooler service, then retry.
Wireless printer not foundThe device is on a different subnet or guest network.Confirm the printer and computer are on the same Wi-Fi network.
Scanner not recognisedThe scanner driver was not installed alongside the print driver.Install the full Ricoh driver package, not the print-only version.

Best Practices

A short checklist to keep your Ricoh printer healthy and your driver in good shape.

  • Always download from Ricoh's own support website rather than third-party mirrors.
  • Match the driver to the exact model number printed on the device — similar models often need different drivers.
  • Install Windows updates first, then the Ricoh driver, so the driver loads against the current print stack.
  • Keep the firmware on the printer itself up to date; firmware and driver are designed as a pair.
  • Reboot the computer after a major driver upgrade to release the old kernel-mode print module.
