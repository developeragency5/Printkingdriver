Scanner Driver

Captures images and handles OCR exchange.

What Is It?

Scanner drivers allow your system to capture images and documents from a scanner. They enable OCR technology, converting scanned text into editable digital formats.

They ensure high-quality scans, fast processing, and compatibility with software. Updated drivers improve accuracy and performance.

How It Works

An application requests a scan through an interface like TWAIN or WIA. The driver instructs the scanner to move the imaging sensor across the page, collects the pixel data, and delivers a finished image back to the application.

Key Functions

  • Initiates and controls scan jobs at chosen resolutions.
  • Selects colour mode, contrast, and brightness.
  • Provides previews and cropping support.
  • Bridges hardware to OCR and document software.

Why It Matters

A reliable scanner driver decides how accurate, fast, and consistent your scans look. Without it, documents arrive blurred, skewed, or never reach the application at all.

Recognising the symptom is the first step in narrowing down whether the problem really is the driver, the hardware or another part of the system.

Best Practices

A short checklist to keep this driver healthy and reduce the chance of running into the issues above.

  • Install both TWAIN and WIA drivers if available — different applications use different APIs.
  • For text documents, scan at 300 DPI; for archival or photos, 600 DPI gives a good balance.
  • Match OCR language settings to the document for accurate text recognition.
  • Clean the glass or feed rollers before assuming the driver is at fault.
  • Keep scanner firmware in step with the driver to retain feeder and duplex features.
